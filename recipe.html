UPMA

this is one of my fav recpie which my always do

Ingredients

  • Semolina (Rava)
  • Water
  • Oil or Ghee
  • Mustard seeds
  • Urad dal (split black gram)
  • Chana dal (split Bengal gram)
  • Green chilies
  • Ginger
  • Curry leaves
  • Onion
  • Salt
  • Vegetables (optional: carrots, peas, beans)
  • Coriander leaves (for garnish)
  • Lemon juice (optional)

Instructions

  1. Roast the Semolina (Rava): Dry roast 1 cup of semolina in a pan on medium heat until it turns light golden and gives a nutty aroma. Remove and keep aside.
  2. Heat Oil/Ghee: In the same pan, heat 2 tablespoons of oil or ghee.
  3. Add Tempering: Add 1 teaspoon mustard seeds and let them splutter. Then add 1 teaspoon urad dal and 1 teaspoon chana dal. Sauté until golden brown.
  4. Add Aromatics: Add 1-2 chopped green chilies, 1 teaspoon grated ginger, and a few curry leaves. Sauté briefly. Add 1 medium chopped onion and cook until translucent.
  5. Add Vegetables (Optional): Add finely chopped vegetables like carrots, peas, or beans if using. Sauté for a few minutes until slightly tender.
  6. Add Water and Salt: Pour in 2.5 to 3 cups of water depending on the desired consistency. Add salt to taste. Bring it to a boil.
  7. Add Roasted Semolina: Gradually add the roasted semolina while continuously stirring to avoid lumps.
  8. Cook and Cover: Stir well, cover with a lid, and cook on low flame for 3-4 minutes until the water is absorbed and the upma is fluffy.
  9. Finish and Garnish: Turn off the heat. Add chopped coriander leaves and a squeeze of lemon juice if desired.
  10. Serve Hot: Serve warm with coconut chutney or enjoy it plain.
